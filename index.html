Our mission

DownStreams is a non-profit social enterprise that uses an online platform to help to build connections between communities along a river

Downstream communities can be affected by floods, environmental pollution & biodiversity issues from their river. Upstream communities are often the source of these issues, but do not have the capability to create change & improve the situation.

Downstreams' aim is to alleviate these problems by joining individuals & businesses along the river together both socially & financially - to look after their river and protect themselves, just as they are joined together by the water that passes them by.

Downstreams relies on the provision of open data about geography as well as information about risks & impact to communities from flooding, biodiversity loss, pollution and about projects that might partially mitigate them.

How we work

We have a different perspective on catchment management - for the environment and for people, joining up concerns about flood prevention, pollution prevention & biodiversity.

We take a joined up view of the ecosystems around a river, taking nature concerns & flood resilience concerns together when trying to find the best solutions. It's clear to us that the best solutions do not come from professionals who are a long way from the catchment, but from people who live in the area and know the local environment.

Our role is not to deliver solutions, but to explain problems associated with the river in an area and elicit solutions that contribute towards solving these problems. We specifically look for solutions that can prevent the problem rather than mitigating impacts.
