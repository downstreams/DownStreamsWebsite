Save the River Worth

Your river is muddy, silted up & has excessive nitrate pollution

The River Worth has excessive mud in the water, due to cattle being allowed to wade in it in some areas. This can cause silt to be washed downstream, making flooding worse. In addition, nitrate pollution from cow manure being washed downstream is causing the river to have too many nutrients, impacting biodiversity, fish & other ecology.

Silting due to cattle

Due to a lack of fencing on some parts of the river, there is a problem with silt being washed downstream, increasing flood risk, blanketing the riverbed with a layer of mud & reducing the oxygen in the water.

Diffuse Nitrate Pollution

Due to many causes including a lack of cattle fencing along sections of the river, manure is washing into the river, raising nitrate levels & increasing the chance of poisonous algal blooms on quiet sections.

Reduced Biodiversity

Silt and nitrate pollution as well as trampling from cattle are seriously impacting the biodiversity of the river, with less fish & vulnerable species being counted in it than there should be.
